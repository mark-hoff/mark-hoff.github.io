Bio

Mark Hoff is Assistant Professor of Spanish in the Department of Hispanic Languages & Literatures at Queens College CUNY, where he teaches graduate and undergraduate courses in Spanish linguistics.

Mark specializes in morphosyntactic and pragmatic variation in Spanish, Portuguese, and other Romance languages. His research adopts a usage-based approach and employs experimental and corpus methods to uncover the contextual conditioning of linguistic variation. Recent work focuses on tense/mood variation, clitic expression and object marking, and pragmatic features of Argentine youth language.

Originally from northern Indiana, Mark attended Indiana University where he first received a dual BA in Spanish and Portuguese, with a minor in Linguistics (2011), followed by an MA in Hispanic Linguistics (2014). Mark then moved to Columbus, Ohio and earned a PhD in Hispanic Linguistics at The Ohio State University (2019). His dissertation was entitled Settledness and mood variation: A semantic-pragmatic analysis of Spanish future-framed adverbials.

Throughout his academic career, Mark has spent extensive time studying and conducting research in Argentina, and he returns as often as possible.
